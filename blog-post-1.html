Monsoon Woes? A Paws-itive Guide to a Happy, Healthy Rainy Season

Posted on September 5, 2025

The arrival of the monsoon brings relief from the heat, but for our furry friends, it can also bring a host of challenges. The constant dampness, lack of outdoor exercise, and booming thunderstorms can be tough on a dog's health and mood.

At Friends' Kennel Hub, we believe a little preparation goes a long way. Here is our expert guide to navigating the rainy season and ensuring your canine companion stays comfortable, healthy, and happy.

1. Paw Care is Paramount

Your dog's paws are on the front lines, constantly exposed to wet, muddy streets. This makes them a breeding ground for fungal and bacterial infections.

What to do:

  • Wipe and Dry: After every walk, no matter how short, gently wash your dogâ€™s paws with lukewarm water and pat them completely dry with a dedicated towel. Pay special attention to the area between the paw pads.
  • Keep Nails Trimmed: Long nails can trap moisture and dirt. Regular trimming helps keep paws clean and healthy.
  • Check for Redness: Regularly inspect their paws for any signs of redness, itching, or irritation. If you notice anything unusual, it's best to consult a vet.

2. Beat the Itch: Coat Care

A damp coat is not just uncomfortable; it can lead to nasty skin infections and that persistent "wet dog" smell.

What to do:

  • Thorough Drying: Every time your dog gets wet, towel-dry them thoroughly. For dogs with thicker coats, a quick once-over with a hairdryer on a low-heat setting can make a huge difference.
  • Regular Brushing: Brushing helps to remove loose fur and dirt, improves air circulation to the skin, and prevents matting, which can trap moisture.
  • Avoid Over-Bathing: While it might seem counterintuitive, too much bathing can strip the natural oils from their coat, making them more susceptible to skin issues. Stick to their regular bathing schedule unless they get excessively dirty.

3. Indoor Enrichment: Beating the Boredom

Shorter walks mean your dog has excess energy to burn. A bored dog is often a destructive dog, so it's important to keep them mentally and physically stimulated indoors.

What to do:

  • Puzzle Toys: Food-dispensing toys and puzzles are a fantastic way to engage their brain and make them work for their treats.
  • Play a Game of "Find It": Hide treats around the house and encourage your dog to use their powerful sense of smell to find them.
  • Training Sessions: The monsoon is the perfect time to brush up on old commands or teach them a new trick. A 15-minute training session can be just as tiring as a short walk.

4. Navigating Noisy Nights: Thunderstorm Anxiety

The loud clap of thunder can be terrifying for many dogs. Creating a safe and calm environment can help ease their anxiety.

What to do:

  • Create a Safe Haven: Make sure they have access to a comfortable, den-like space, such as their crate or a quiet corner with their favourite blanket.
  • Provide Distractions: Playing calming music or turning on the TV can help mask the noise from outside. A long-lasting chew toy can also be a great distraction.
  • Stay Calm: Your dog looks to you for reassurance. Act normally and calmly, and give them gentle pets if they seek comfort, but avoid excessive coddling, which can reinforce their fear.

By following these simple tips, you can ensure the monsoon season is a happy and stress-free time for both you and your beloved pet. For any specific health concerns, don't hesitate to reach out to our team at Friends' Kennel Hub for expert guidance and vet consultations!
